High Altitude Balloon

During a typical balloon flight, a payload will be exposed to atmospheric pressures of less than a percent of those at sea level and temperatures of -50Â°C. Mission times are usually over 90 minutes.

The high altitude balloon team is developing a sustainable platform for near space research experiments. This will provide an opportunity for internal and external projects to have low cost access to space.

HAB DESIGN

AVIONICS

The electronics unit is controlled by three Arduino Nanos functioning as its main processing units. Each controller manages one of three things: telemetry, data handling and storage or peripheral sensing and flight stage operation handling. The telemetry controller also communicates with a GPS allowing for accurate identification of location. Furthermore, the peripheral sensing unit includes attitude determination functionality which can be utilised in attitude control if the need arises.

STRUCTURE

The HAB module undergoes minimal external forces during operation and therefore the priority of the structure is to be lightweight and minimise the damage caused to surroundings upon landing. A polystyrene 1.4 litre container will sufficiently protect components from low temperatures, soften the landing impact (with the help of a parachute) and increase the altitude the HAB will atain.
