Project Nova.

SunSat aims to have a satellite (Nova-1) in orbit by 2025. Nova-1 will monitor the rate of deforestation from Low Earth Orbit (LEO) by carrying a highresolution camera to facilitate Earth Observation.

Nova-1 will be a demonstration that a student-built satellite is feasible. Future Nova satellites will be designed with flexibility and modularity in mind which means that the same core systems can be used with a variety of experiments with little to no alteration, enabling fast mission turnarounds.

NOVA DESIGN

AVIONICS

The electronics unit is controlled by three Arduino Nanos functioning as its main processing units. Each controller manages one of three things: telemetry, data handling and storage or peripheral sensing and flight stage operation handling. The telemetry controller also communicates with a GPS allowing for accurate identification of location. Furthermore, the peripheral sensing unit includes attitude determination functionality which can be utilised in attitude control if the need arises.

STRUCTURES

The NOVA module is designed to be compatible with the payload deployment system within Sunride’s Vesna rocket. The structure’s primary objective is to support the internal components through the harsh acceleration of up to 20Gs, and vibration during launch. The NOVA structure is a modified 2.5U (10cm x 10cm x 25cm) satellite manufactured from aluminium with an outer surface of 3D printed PLA.

Spaceport America Cup

A prototype version of Nova-1 is in currently in the build phase and will be flown this summer at the Spaceport America Cup, a large spaceflight competition, in New Mexico.
